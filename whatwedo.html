Year 1: Foundation Courses


Mathematics and Science:

Students take foundational courses in mathematics and science, including calculus, physics, and chemistry, to build a strong mathematical and scientific background.

Programming Fundamentals:

ntroduction to programming languages like C++ or Java, covering basic programming concepts and problem-solving skills.

Digital Logic Design:

Learning the fundamentals of digital circuits, Boolean algebra, and basic logic gates.

Engineering and Computer Science Fundamentals:

Introduction to engineering principles, computer architecture, and data structures.

Year 2: Building Technical Skills


Data Structures and Algorithms:

More advanced programming topics focusing on data structures and algorithm design.

Electronics and Circuit Analysis:

In-depth study of electronics, including analog and digital circuits.

Computer Organization and Architecture:

Understanding the internal components and organization of computer systems.

Operating Systems:

Learning about the theory and operation of operating systems like Windows, Linux, and macOS.

Year 3: Specialization and Advanced Topics


Computer Networks:

Studying how computer networks operate, including network protocols and design.

Embedded Systems:

Focusing on designing and programming embedded systems, often used in devices like microcontrollers and IoT devices.

Software Engineering:

Advanced software development methodologies, software testing, and project management.

Year 3: On-the-JobTraining = 240 Hours


On-the-JobTraining

Some programs require students to complete an internship, gaining real-world experience in a professional engineering or IT environment.

Year 4: Integration and Capstone Project


Capstone Project:

Students often work on a major project that demonstrates their knowledge and skills in a practical context. This project can range from designing a software application to building a hardware system.

Elective Courses:

Depending on their interests, students can choose elective courses in areas such as artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, robotics, or computer graphics.

Professional Development:

Preparing for the transition to the workforce, including job search strategies, resume writing, and interview preparation.
